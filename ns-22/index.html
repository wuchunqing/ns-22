ACSM Official Electrolyte Guidelines

  • Sodium: 300-1000 mg/hour (prevents cramps & hyponatremia)
  • Potassium: 80-200 mg/hour (muscle function)
  • Magnesium: 10-30 mg/hour (nerve & muscle health)
  • Calcium: 100-300 mg/hour (bone & contraction)
  • Pair with 500-750ml water per hour for optimal absorption

Electrolyte Replacement FAQ

When do I need electrolyte replacement?

For all workouts >60 minutes, especially in heat/humidity – replaces sweat loss to avoid cramps, fatigue & hyponatremia.

Why is sodium the most important?

Sodium makes up 90% of sweat electrolytes; it regulates fluid balance and is the primary cause of exercise cramps.

Can I over-supplement electrolytes?

Yes – excessive sodium causes bloating. Stick to ACSM limits for your workout duration & intensity.

Electrolyte Replacement Pro Tips

  • Start replacing 30 mins into long workouts
  • Prioritize sodium for endurance >90 mins
  • Avoid sugary drinks – slow electrolyte absorption
  • Test drinks in training, not race day
  • Add salt tabs for ultra-distance events

Signs of Electrolyte Deficiency

  • Muscle cramps (sodium/potassium deficit)
  • Fatigue & dizziness (sodium loss)
  • Nausea (hyponatremia)
  • Weakness (magnesium deficit)
